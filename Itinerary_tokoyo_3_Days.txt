--- Planning Day 1 ---
Here is a suggested itinerary for Day 1 in Tokyo:

**Morning:** Shibuya Crossing (9:00 am - 10:30 am)

Start your day by exploring one of Tokyo's most iconic landmarks, Shibuya Crossing. Take a walk around the famous scramble crossing and take in the sights and sounds of this bustling area. You can also grab a coffee or breakfast at one of the many cafes nearby.

**Afternoon:** Tsukiji Fish Market (11:00 am - 1:30 pm)

Head to Tsukiji Fish Market, one of the world's largest fish markets, to experience the tuna auction market (if you're there early enough) and sample some fresh sushi at Sushi Dai or Daiwa Sushi. Be sure to arrive by 11:00 am to make the most of your visit.

**Evening:** Asakusa's Senso-ji Temple (3:00 pm - 5:30 pm)

After lunch, head to Asakusa's historic district and explore Nakamise Shopping Street, a traditional shopping street filled with snacks and souvenirs. This will lead you to Senso-ji Temple, an ancient Buddhist temple famous for its beautiful architecture and vibrant atmosphere.

This itinerary provides a great balance of culture, food, and entertainment while avoiding any activities from the previous day. Enjoy your first day in Tokyo!

--- Planning Day 2 ---
Here is a suggested itinerary for Day 2 in Tokyo:

**Morning:** Tokyo Skytree (9:00 am - 11:30 am)

Start your day by visiting Tokyo Skytree, the tallest broadcasting tower in the world. Take the elevator to the observation deck at an impressive 450 meters high and enjoy panoramic views of the city. You can also learn about the history and construction of the tower through interactive exhibits.

**Afternoon:** Akihabara Electric Town (12:00 pm - 3:00 pm)

Head to Akihabara, Tokyo's electronics and anime hub, known for its quirky shops and otaku culture. Explore the various electronic stores, such as Yodobashi Akiba or Mandarake, which sell everything from gadgets to collectible figurines. Don't miss the iconic Maneki-Neko (Lucky Cat) statue outside one of the famous electronic shops.

**Evening:** Meiji Shrine (4:00 pm - 6:30 pm)

After lunch and some shopping in Akihabara, head to Meiji Shrine, a serene Shinto shrine dedicated to the deified spirits of Emperor Meiji and his wife, Empress Shoken. Take a peaceful stroll through the beautiful gardens and enjoy the tranquil atmosphere.

This itinerary provides a great mix of modern technology, unique culture, and relaxation, while avoiding any activities from the previous day. Enjoy your second day in Tokyo!

--- Planning Day 3 ---
Here is a suggested itinerary for Day 3 in Tokyo:

Start your day by experiencing the busiest and most iconic intersection in the world, Shibuya Crossing. Take a stroll around the Shibuya area and capture some photos of the famous scramble crossing. You can also visit the Shibuya 109 department store, which is a popular shopping destination for young fashionistas.

**Afternoon:** Asakusa's Senso-ji Temple (12:00 pm - 3:30 pm)

Head to Asakusa's historic district and visit Senso-ji Temple, one of the oldest and most famous Buddhist temples in Japan. Explore the colorful Nakamise Shopping Street leading up to the temple, which is filled with traditional snack stalls, souvenir shops, and street performers.

**Evening:** Tsukiji Fish Market (4:00 pm - 7:00 pm)

Finish your day by exploring the world-famous Tsukiji Fish Market. While the tuna auction has moved to a new location, you can still experience the sights and smells of the market at the outer market stalls. Sample some fresh sushi at Sushi Dai or Daiwa Sushi, and explore the various food stalls and shops selling everything from seafood to kitchenware.

This itinerary provides a mix of modern entertainment, cultural experiences, and culinary delights, while avoiding any activities from Days 1 and 2. Enjoy your third day in Tokyo!
